Fundamentals of Faith

Lesson 3 – The Attributes of God - Additional Notes

## **Faithfulness**

> What does faithfulness mean? – true to one’s word – but more than
> honesty – there seems to be a moral sense to it also
>
> Psalm 36:5 – Your lovingkindness, O Lord, extends to the heavens,
> **<u>Your faithfulness reaches to the skies</u>**.
>
> What is the extent of God’s faithfulness? – it reaches to the skies
>
> What does that mean? – it is endless
>
> <u>2 Timothy 2:13</u> – If we are faithless, **<u>He remains
> faithful</u>**, for **<u>He cannot deny Himself</u>**.

Does God withhold His faithfulness when we are faithless? – No.

Why not? – He cannot deny Himself.

What does that mean? – He is obligated to keep His promises

## **Immutability**

> What does immutability mean? – unchanging, unchangeable
>
> <u>Deuteronomy 32:4</u> – **<u>The Rock</u>**! His work is perfect,
> for all His ways are just; a God of faithfulness and without
> injustice, righteous and upright is He
>
> How is God referred to here? – as a rock
>
> What does a rock symbolize? – firmness, lasting, unbreakable
>
> <u>Malachi 3:6</u> – For **<u>I, the Lord, do not change</u>**;
> therefore you, O sons of Jacob, are not consumed.
>
> What does the Lord say about Himself here? – He does not change
>
> <u>James 1:17</u> – Every good thing given and every perfect gift is
> from above, coming down from **<u>the Father of lights, with whom
> there is no variation or shifting shadow</u>**.

Who is the subject of this verse? – the Father of Lights

Who is that? – God

What is said about the Father of Lights? – He does not vary or shift
like a shadow

> What should that give us when we read our Bibles of all the accounts
> of God’s acts and promises for thousands of years? – confidence;
> trust; assurance.
>
> Finally – Hebrews 13:8 – Jesus Christ is the same yesterday, today,
> and forever.
>
> How does your heart/soul/spirit react when you think that God never
> changes? OPEN.
